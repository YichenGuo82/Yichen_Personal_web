Georgetown University | MSDSA - Adv Data Visualization Final Project

WOW, fantastic city! But where should I stay?

Imagine you are planning a trip to Washington, D.C.. In order to immerse yourself in the local culture, you have decided to book a house on Airbnb. To ensure that you have the best possible experience, you are carefully reviewing all available information, including reviews, maps, prices and other details, before making your final selection.

By Yifan Bian, Yichen Guo, Ting Huang, Xingyang Liu

Intro

Airbnb has undoubtedly become the go-to platform for travelers seeking a comfortable and authentic experience when visiting new places. For both the Washington D.C. community and travel enthusiasts exploring the continent, Airbnb offers a wide range of accommodations to suit every taste and budget.

In this visual exploration, we will guide you through the diverse Airbnb listings available in and around the nation's capital, delving into the different room types, factors influencing price, and guest reviews. Join us in uncovering the secrets to finding the perfect Airbnb listing and learn how to make the most of your next visit to this historic city.

Our goals are:

1. Let readers understand the business status of Airbnb through data visualization, hoping that the audience can interact with our visual charts to better understand the various Airbnb room types in the District of Columbia.

2. Explore which factors are related to Airbnb pricing and which factors are irrelevant through data science research.

Analysis

Room Type distribution

Figure 1:Interactive Map of Airbnb Listings in Washington, D.C.

Figure 2: Distribution of Room Types in Washington, D.C.

The plot clearly illustrates that the majority of Airbnb listings in Washington D.C. are Entire home/apartment options, accounting for 75.3% of the available accommodations. Meanwhile, 22.8% of hosts offer private rooms, generating additional income by sharing their living spaces with guests. Interestingly, only a small fraction of the listings, 104 out of 6,455 (1.6%), involve hosts sharing a room with strangers. Even though, this is more closely aligned with the original concept of couch sharing upon which Airbnb was founded.

Price Factor

Price has always been one of the most important factors for people who use airbnb, and we used several charts to analyze what price factors are associated with and not associated with.

Figure 3:Median Listing Prices by Neighbourhood

We use the median to mitigate the impact of extreme values. Notably, the neighborhoods of Downtown, Chinatown, Penn Quarters, Mount Vernon Square, and North Capitol Street command the highest listing prices in Washington D.C. Furthermore, listings located in the inner circle of our site tend to be more expensive than those in the outer circle.

Figure 4:Relationship between room price and number of reviews, number of days available and minimum number of nights

The second chart shows the relationship between price and number of reviews, days available, and minimum days. This is contrary to our previous prediction. Before the analysis, we believed that more days available may be negatively correlated with price while the minimum days may be positively correlated with price. As it turns out, there is little linear relationship between price and these factors. We believe that the price may be related to the cost of room decoration, service quality and room size. These figures, however, are hard to come by and are merely predictions.

Figure 5:Calendar Heatmap of Average Prices from Mar 2022 to Mar 2023

This plot represents the average price of all listings on a single day, with each block representing a separate day. The darkness of the color corresponds to the price, with darker shades indicating higher prices. This calendar heatmap tracks the daily average price changes over 12 months. Of particular interest is the fact that Thursdays and Fridays consistently exhibit darker colors, indicating higher prices on these days.

See Pattern See Relationship

Figure 6 & 7: Average Listing Prices (Left) and Average Review Rate (Right) from May 2022 to May 2023 with LOESS Trendline

Since data on occupancy is unavailable, we computed the review rate by dividing the number of reviews by the number of listings on a given day. This allowed us to estimate the level of demand in the market.

The two plots can be viewed as representations of the supply and demand of listings. After applying LOESS, we observe similar seasonal trends in both plots. Specifically, both the average price and the average comment rate exhibit peaks in October and a decline from November to January. This suggests that the price fluctuations have a seasonal pattern that can be partly attributed to changes in demand.

If we filter the review ratings to only include those above 0.05, a surprising result emerges. After filtering for higher review rates, we discovered that the average price on the corresponding day was consistently lower than that of the surrounding days. This indicates that the market's supply and demand dynamics change quickly. So if you come across a reasonably priced listing, we recommend making a decision quickly, as competition is likely to be high.

Conclusion

From the above analysis, we can easily draw a conclusion.

First of all, Airbnb has a variety of room types. However, no matter it is based on the values of the enterprise or the preferences of most consumers, in order to protect the privacy of guests, most of the listings are entire home or private room. If you think about it, as consumers, having an independent living environment is also one of the most important factors we need to consider.

In terms of price, Airbnb rooms in the central areas of DC, including tourist areas, urban business districts and other places, have a higher price obviously, which is determined by supply and demand. There is a large demand for rooms in these areas, so the price is naturally high. The warmth of the room (as measured by the number of reviews), the number of days available, and the minimum number of nights had little to do with price.

Finally, the price is also related to the time, because DC is also an important tourist city. Tourism has low season, and the room price is relatively low in the off-season, while the price is higher in the peak season. Finally, we analyzed the correlation between various factors of Airbnb rooms. The location factor has a relatively low relationship with other factors, because other factors, such as kitchen and cleanliness, are usually related to the room quality and the host's service attitude, but have nothing to do with location.

Method

In our study, plotly and altair are mainly used as visualization tools, including map_plot, bar chart, scatter chart, etc., combined with various data analysis packages in python, and finally, data storytelling is used to demonstrate the data story of airbnb in DC to the audience. Our pictures have good visualization effect and strong interactive ability to let readers more efficient access to data information.
